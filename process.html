01
Discovery & Diagnostic

Understand before we advise

We don't come in with pre-formed answers. The first phase is entirely dedicated to understanding your organisation — its history, its people, its current challenges, and where it wants to go.

We combine structured interviews, diagnostic frameworks, and document reviews to build a clear, honest picture of where things stand — including the issues that often go unspoken.

Typical Duration2–4 weeks depending on org size
Key StakeholdersFounders, senior leadership, key functional heads
DeliverableDiagnostic report with prioritised findings
1

Stakeholder Interviews

Structured conversations with founders, leaders, and key team members to surface the real state of the organisation — not just the official narrative.

2

Document & Data Review

Review of org charts, job descriptions, performance data, hiring records, and any existing people frameworks to understand what exists and what's missing.

3

Diagnostic Report

A clear summary of findings: what's working, what's creating friction, and which people challenges are most urgently connected to your business goals.

02
Strategy Design

A talent roadmap built for your specific context

Based on diagnostic findings, we co-design a talent strategy that is specific, prioritised, and connected to real business objectives — not a generic HR playbook.

This is a collaborative phase. You know your business better than anyone; we bring the frameworks, the challenge, and the outside perspective needed to build something genuinely useful.

Typical Duration3–6 weeks, including workshops
Key StakeholdersFounders, CEO, CHRO or Head of People
DeliverableTalent strategy roadmap with 90-day priorities
1

Priority Setting Workshop

A facilitated session to align leadership on the 3–5 highest-leverage people priorities for the next 12 months — and build shared commitment to acting on them.

2

Framework Development

Design of the specific frameworks, structures, or systems needed — whether that's a performance model, a hiring process, a values framework, or a succession map.

3

Roadmap & Success Metrics

A phased implementation plan with clear milestones, owners, and ways of measuring whether the strategy is actually working.

03
Implementation Support

Turning strategy into practice — together

Strategy only creates value when it's implemented. We work alongside your team during the execution phase to ensure frameworks are adopted, not just delivered.

The level of hands-on support is calibrated to your team's capacity and capability — from light-touch advisory to deep co-implementation across multiple functions.

Typical Duration6–16 weeks depending on scope
Key StakeholdersPeople team, managers, functional leads
DeliverableLive systems, trained teams, embedded practices
1

Manager & Team Enablement

Workshops and coaching sessions to help managers and HR teams understand, adopt, and confidently apply the new frameworks in their day-to-day work.

2

Live Integration

Support as frameworks go live — sitting in on hiring panels, reviewing performance conversations, facilitating leadership sessions — to ensure quality and catch issues early.

3

Documentation & Handover

All frameworks, processes, and toolkits are fully documented so your team can run them independently long after the engagement ends.

04
Review & Iteration

Continuous improvement, not a one-time fix

People strategy isn't a project with a finish line. Organisations change, markets shift, and the frameworks you build need to evolve alongside them.

The review phase builds in regular reflection points to assess what's working, what needs adjusting, and whether the organisation's people challenges have changed since we began.

CadenceQuarterly check-ins, annual strategy reviews
Success MetricMeasurable outcomes vs. original baseline
DeliverableProgress report and next-phase recommendations
1

Outcome Measurement

Track agreed metrics — hiring speed, retention, engagement scores, promotion rates — against the baseline established during the diagnostic phase.

2

Leadership Review Sessions

Quarterly strategic conversations to share observations, surface emerging challenges, and keep leadership aligned on people priorities as the business evolves.

3

Iteration & Next Phase

Recommendations for what to refine, what to add, and which new challenges are ready to be addressed — keeping the people agenda connected to where the business is going.

Common Questions

How long does a typical engagement take?

Most project-based engagements run between 8–20 weeks depending on scope and complexity. Retainer arrangements are ongoing month-to-month with a minimum 3-month commitment. We always provide a clear scope and timeline before any work begins.

Do you work with early-stage startups or only established businesses?

Both. We have worked with companies from Series A through to multi-hundred-person organisations, as well as family businesses and founder-led companies at various stages. The approach is always calibrated to the organisation's stage and capacity.

What does working with you look like day-to-day?

It depends on the engagement, but typically involves regular video or in-person sessions (weekly or fortnightly), asynchronous collaboration on documents and frameworks, facilitated workshops with your team, and ongoing availability for ad-hoc questions via email or messaging.

Can we start with just one practice area?

Absolutely. Many clients start with a single, high-priority area and expand the scope over time. A discovery call will help us identify which area will have the highest near-term impact for your organisation.

What if we already have an internal HR team?

We work closely with internal HR and People teams — not around them. Our role is to bring an outside perspective, specialised frameworks, and additional capacity. Most of our most successful engagements have been in partnership with strong internal HR leads.
